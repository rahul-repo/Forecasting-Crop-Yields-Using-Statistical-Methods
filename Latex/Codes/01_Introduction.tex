\section{Introduction}
Agriculture has historically been the backbone of the Indian economy. Despite its declining share in GDP (approximately 18 \% in 2023), it remains the primary source of livelihood for nearly 45 \% of the population. India's agricultural system is a complex interplay of traditional practices, policy interventions, technological shifts, and socio-environmental dynamics. This paper explores different factors that affects the crop yield of India and predict the future production of Indian agriculture through a multidisciplinary lens.
