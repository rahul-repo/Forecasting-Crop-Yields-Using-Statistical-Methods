\section{Conclusion}

This project developed predictive models to estimate crop yield using 25 years of agricultural and environmental data. Both Gaussian Ridge Regression and Random Forest models were employed, with the Ridge model exhibiting superior performance due to the underlying linear structure of the data. Key findings revealed that average temperature, fertilizer usage, and technology index were the most influential factors affecting yield.

Bayesian optimization was applied to determine the optimal allocation of fertilizer and technology investment under budget constraints, resulting in a 9.7\% improvement in predicted yield. These results highlight the effectiveness of combining statistical modeling with optimization techniques for informed agricultural decision-making.
